# The Punch Bank

*What we're actually building, and why it's better than points.*

---

## The problem we refused to accept

Buy-ten-get-one-free has one fatal move baked into it: **the reset.**

You show up, you're a stranger. You come back nine times, you become somebody — the card fills, the finish line gets close, it *means* something. Then you redeem, and the machine takes it all away and drops you back to zero. Stranger again. The one moment you did everything right — you gave a shop ten visits — is the exact moment it demotes you.

That's not a loyalty program. That's a slot machine that resets your winnings every time you win.

Points were supposed to be the upgrade. And people *say* they love points. But nobody has ever felt a flicker of joy calculating "2.3 stars per dollar." What people actually love isn't the math — it's the **score.** A number that's *theirs.* One that climbs and never falls. One that offers a choice when it's time to spend. One that occasionally, unpredictably, gives them a little more than they earned.

Here's the thing the big chains will never say out loud: **every property that makes points feel good is computable from visits alone.** You don't need to know what someone ordered. You don't need a POS integration. You need a number that goes up and never comes down.

So we're taking the score — the *good* part of points — and throwing away the casino.

## The one-sentence version

**Punches are points with the casino removed.**

Everything people quietly hate about points — expiry, silent devaluation, opaque ratios, "we make money when you forget to redeem" — lives in the part Punched literally cannot build: the dollar linkage. We're not being noble by leaving it out. We *can't* put it in. Which means our biggest limitation is also the most honest loyalty product on the market. **The weakness is the brand.**

## Why Starbucks can't copy this

This isn't bravado. It's structural.

- Their earning is **spend-based.** A visit-based bank breaks the entire economic model that ties rewards to how much money left your wallet.
- Their P&L quietly depends on **breakage** — the points you earn and never redeem. Kill expiry and "your balance is always yours, forever" and you blow a hole in the spreadsheet.
- They can't print the joke. We can say "Big numbers just feel nice. We checked." Their legal and finance teams would have a stroke, because their whole system is built on you *not* doing the math.

We win here precisely because we're small, visit-based, and we actually give a shit. Those are the three things they can't become.

## The machine: how the Punch Bank works

**The pass shows a balance, not a fraction.** Never "7/10." Always "Balance: 14 punches." It climbs. It never resets. Ever.

**The owner sets a tiny menu, once.** Not a "system," not "tiers" — a menu, in their own words: *what can your regulars earn?* Two or three rungs, pre-filled off their real menu so it's look-right-tap-yes:

- ~4 punches → a small treat (cookie, drip coffee)
- ~10 punches → the classic (any drink)
- ~20 punches → the big one (bag of beans, merch)

The setup wizard enforces one rule quietly: **value per punch can never get worse as you climb.** The big prize is always at least as good, per punch, as the small one. No bait-and-switch ladders. The customer can always trust that going higher is never a trap.

**Crossing a rung is a moment.** The instant a check-in puts someone at or past a reward, the post-scan screen gives them a real choice:

> **CASH OUT** — free drink, right now.
> **LET IT RIDE** — 6 more punches and the whole bag of beans is yours.
>
> *Riding can't lose. Your free coffee stays banked either way. This isn't Vegas. It's a bakery.*

That last line is the whole philosophy in three sentences. In a casino, letting it ride means you can lose what you had. Here, **there is no losing.** The lower reward stays unlocked and claimable forever. "Let it ride" just means "not yet." We made the phrase safe. That's the trick the big boys can't pull off, because their version of "let it ride" — points expiring, tiers demoting — actually *can* take it away from you.

**The iron rules** (these are load-bearing — they're what makes it trustworthy):

1. **Unlocked rungs are claimable forever.** Once you can afford the cookie, you can always get the cookie.
2. **Partial spend keeps the remainder banked.** Redeem the 10-punch drink out of a 14 balance, you walk away with 4 still in the bank. No zeroing.
3. **Silence = banked.** Do nothing and nothing is lost. The default is always in the customer's favor.

**Redemption is a ticket, not a tap.** When someone cashes out, the screen becomes a full-screen, timed ticket the staff can see and verify — a clean hand-off moment at the counter, hard to fake, easy to honor.

## Where this goes (the part that gets big)

The Punch Bank is a *chassis*, not a one-off. Once a balance is a real, banked, never-resetting number, everything else clips on:

- **Moe Money** — show the balance ×100 and confess the joke. "Big numbers just feel nice. We checked." *(shipped — Wave 1)*
- **The Odometer** — "Visit #47," ranks from Newcomer to Legend, and we never demote a regular. "Cards reset. You don't." *(shipped — Wave 1)*
- **Name Your Prize** — ask what they're working toward; every update counts down to *their* oat-milk mocha. Personalization with zero surveillance: "We don't spy on your orders. We asked."
- **Lucky Punch** — some self-scans land double. Odds printed in plain English, a pity rule so it always eventually hits. It only ever *gives.*
- **Moe's Tokens** — the same ledger, one day, goes town-wide. Every shop funds only redemptions at its own counter, so there's no inter-shop settlement nightmare — but a local's balance becomes a passport across every Punched shop in town. A brand-new shop inherits day-one loyal regulars. *That's* the merchant pitch.

## The rule we will not break

Three voices, kept strictly apart:

- **The owner** hears plain shop words — punches, reward, regulars. Never our feature names, never "tiers" or "systems." Setup is one friendly question with the blanks already filled in.
- **The customer** hears Moe — Moe Money, Furniture, the manifesto, the roast. All the fun lives here.
- **The codenames** — Punch Bank, Odometer, Wave 2 — never appear anywhere a human can see them.

And Moe ships standard on every card. There's no personality toggle, no "corporate mode." The brand *is* the product. A shop owner who wanted a beige, soulless loyalty punch card would have bought a beige, soulless loyalty punch card. They came to Punched.
